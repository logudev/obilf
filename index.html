    About the Foundation

    OUR MODEL
    • Sign MOUs with Block Education Officers
    • Adopt rural Government Primary schools
    • Identify educated / semi-educated homemakers from the same communities to train and employ them as Coordinators to teach English & Computers to the children of these schools
    • Bring resources like Laptops, Tablets and Libraries
    • Rural Coordinators receive weekly in-house training
    • Rural Coordinators also undergo training in other Institutions
    • Volunteers from the city visit the schools once a week to interact with the kids and the Coordinators
    • Kids & Coordinators are assessed regularly to gauge the impact of OBLF intervention

    OUR STRENGTHS
    • Partnering the Government by using the existing infrastructure.
    • Activity based, Participatory and fun way of learning
    • IT utilities like Laptops & Tablets introduced to Rural Children
    • Measurable Outcomes (Through a Baseline Assessment System which determines the childâ€™s level of comfort with the language, as well as measures the Impact of our Intervention)
    • Cost per child less than Rs.3,000/- p.a.
    • Management costs well under 5 %
    • Average Teacher / Student ratio is 1:25
    • 5 week Summer Tutorials for continuity and focused learning
    • Empowerment of rural women without disturbing the rural ethos
    • Weekly in-house training of Rural Coordinators
    • Rural Coordinators trained in other organisations, increasing their levels of self-confidence.
    • Independent, Volunteer Professionals from varying fields as Trustees and Director

    Less..
    OUR RELEVANCE
    • Over 50,000 primary schools in Karnataka run by the state government and over 5.6 million children attend these schools.
    • But the ASER tells us that the quality of education is a matter of grave concern.
    • Learning English and Computer skills at an early age help these children to access the information and technology available to others.
    • This will help them find meaningful employment when they reach adulthood.
    • By partnering the Government and bringing new resources to these schools, we are working hard to tap this huge potential by using the existing infrastructure of govt. schools, and building the basic foundation in English & technology in a fun-filled non-prescriptive manner with the help of rural women and volunteers from the city.
